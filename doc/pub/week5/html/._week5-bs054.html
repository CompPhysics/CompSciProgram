Famous PDEs, Laplace's equation

Another familiar equation from electrostatics is Laplace's equation, which looks similar to the wave equation in Eq. (18) except that we have set \( A=0 \)

$$ \begin{equation} \tag{20} \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}+\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2}=0, \end{equation} $$

or if we have a finite electric charge represented by a charge density \( \rho(\mathbf{x}) \) we have the familiar Poisson equation

$$ \begin{equation} \tag{21} \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}+\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2}=-4\pi \rho(\mathbf{x}). \end{equation} $$
